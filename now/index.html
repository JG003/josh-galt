VENTUROPOLY

Frontier Investment Lifestyle

Venturopoly focuses on uncovering innovative ideas and unique opportunities in emerging markets.

We invest time and resources into long-term frontier deals, while simultaneously maximizing personal growth through challenging outdoor adventures and unique lifestyle experiences.

It’s a sustainability-minded vehicle to create positive impact and lasting change while having as much fun as humanly possible exploring this amazing planet we live on.

WHY?   |   MISSION   |   TACTICS

As can be expected with anything frontier or adventure related, Venturopoly started out on a different path than I’m traveling with it now, but through all the changes it’s remained the all-encompassing vehicle driving the next chapter of my life.

Initially, myself and my co-founding partner were going to be creating epic outdoor meetup events for a group of frontier investors, in combination with sourcing deals for them to be pitched on in between adventures.

After a year, my partner needed to focus on other responsibilities for a time, and I decided to go all-in with Entomophagy. Now things are coming back full circle.

Ultimately, while it’s still evolving, Venturopoly is a framework for my life and a structure for the myriad projects that I am currently investing my own time, energy, and resources into.

I’ve been living and traveling in frontier and developing countries since I was born. And while I’m perfectly comfortable with the #luxlifestyle in any first world city, there’s just something I love about the grittiness of a world in the midst of growth chaos.

Exploration can take many forms – while it used to mean I was looking for whitewater rivers in canyons few humans had been down, now the search is to uncover global opportunities from the hinterlands to the largest cultural hubs, and enjoy the process of making the world a better place for every individual person I can reach.

That, combined with enjoying the journey to the fullest in every way, is what Venturopoly was created to be all about.

ENTOPIAN CAPITAL

The insects as feed and food industry has seen rapid changes the past several years, and appears primed for massive development over the next decade and beyond.

I’ve teamed up with other insect-focused experts with a clear vision for the future to form Entopian Capital, a firm that invests via SPV’s in innovative early stage startups with exponential growth potential in the Insects as Feed & Food sector.

Entopian was created based on the belief that a more sustainable food system is necessary, and that insects are a solution to a wide variety of problems, which fall into 3 primary categories:

  • Animal Feed for Aquaculture, Poultry, and Livestock
  • Organic Waste Management and Nutrient Upcycling
  • Human Food Security and Nutrition

Additionally, research into myriad insect by-products shows extensive promise across many industries, taking their potential beyond entomophagy and into an even broader market.

Learn more at Entopian.com.

ENTOVEGAN

I’m putting my theories about edible insects to the test on my own body, living on a plant-based vegan diet boosted by sustainable Entomophagy!

After several years, I’m now fully convinced that vegan + insects is the ideal dietary framework for humans.

I’ve never really dieted publicly before, but I’ve been publishing both the good and the bad and what I’m learning through the process – transparency is the thing these days right? And I’ve actually learned quite a bit from my body and from people’s responses.

Learn more about the Philosophy of Entovegan or check out the site for tips on nutrition, workouts, and other biohacks related to the world’s ultimate sustainable superfood diet: Entovegan.

I’ve always been healthy, but always a carnivore, too! Until one day in early 2017 a friend convinced me to give up meat for a while, and see how I felt after.

The caveat was that I didn’t want to give up eating insects, since I’d taken a liking to them the previous two years, and had jumped fully into the world of entomophagy. So I decided to combine the two, ENTOmophagy and eating VEGAN, and get the best of sustainability and health from the plant-based diet and the improvement nutritionally from eating bugs!

Thus began my journey as an Entovegan, the new portmanteau word I coined to describe the combination of entomophagy with a vegan diet.

I don’t claim to be a vegan who eats insects – no, to be very clear, Entovegan is a completely new word and dietary framework, and it’s most likely the healthiest sustainable diet on the planet.

What the future holds for me and the Entovegan diet remains to be seen, but I feel healthier and stronger than I have in years, and I think my journey with this is just getting started!

LOVE #EATBUGS

BUGS IN YOUR FEELS

As the insect nutrition trend grows, companies that sell insects as food are making a concerted effort to focus on the positive – on the wow factor, not the yuck factor, and on the healthy, sustainable, delicious elements that insects bring to the table.

But I wanted to take it a step further, and create a rebuttal to all the criticisms I’ve heard about Entomophagy, in the most memorable, moving way I could.

I produced the campaign and photographed the images (with exceptions as noted), and somehow I even managed to wiggle my way into several of them haha! I’m also kinda verbose, so each concept or insect will have a corresponding blog post, and new images will be released on a daily basis.

And while the images are strikingly beautiful, most of the people featured are not professional models, but they are all bug-eating enthusiasts.

I wanted the images to be as real as possible, and it’s important for the subject to genuinely be an expert at what they’re doing.

Bugs are still scary for many people, so I was lucky to have some beautiful friends who are comfortable with live insects and also enjoy eating them. It made getting the shots I wanted a lot easier.

FOR THE LOVE OF BUGS!

Insects are incredible – they truly are fascinating and beautiful. But -and this is really important- they’re also the most sustainable protein source we have on the planet right now. Literally.

And so the idea that we should love the planet – that’s pretty easy for people to understand, and the messaging surrounding edible insects has covered that ground quite well.

The idea that everyone should be comfortable in their bodies, that’s a relevant cultural message right now too. But what does that mean, really?

To me, it means being your best self. Not fitting into a mold, but being you, being real, being “more than just exoskeleton deep” – but also being health-conscious.

Insects are one of the healthiest, most environmentally-friendly food options available right now. So if you’re going to take care of your body -your temple, so to speak- then insects are a great source of fuel for it.

And they meet the first qualification better than anything else, being a nearly perfect solution if you love the planet.

Loving your food also means understanding it. And that’s something which, the more I’ve studied these insects, the more I’ve learned about their life cycle and their nutritional qualities for humans, the more I’ve come to truly love them.

Yet I get flak from people who have long been fans of insects as spectacular creatures (entomologists, et al), because they think now that turning them into food will somehow demean them and take away from their unique and varied qualities.

I see it exactly the opposite, because I’ve lived it and nearly every single person I’ve met in the entomophagy industry has a similar story. Turning insects into food – and learning everything there is to learn about them – made me fall in love with bugs in a way I never would have otherwise. Truly.

I think all kinds of animals are cool, but I haven’t really cared about bugs since I was a kids, back living on a farm. As an adult, they’ve always been a nuisance of some sort.

But now that I’m eating them, and re-dedicated to understanding them on the deepest level possible, I’m realizing once again with childlike fascination just how amazing insects truly are, in so many ways.

An entomologist once shared their concern that eating bugs might take away from appreciating them as insects – but this is exactly the point that I want to make.

If we’re going to eat insects, we should love them for everything they are, and for everything they give to our planet and our bodies.

They are beautiful. They are fascinating. And they are surprisingly – and uniquely – delicious.

My goal with this campaign is to bring awareness, to challenge preconceptions, and to re-frame the concept of eating bugs.

To move it away from some gross act you do on a dare, and toward the reality that eating insects is an act of love for the planet, and for yourself.

And if you learn why, then grateful appreciation for the natural beauty of those insects is the result!

CHANGRIT KAMLEANG

100% Natural Cricket Protein powder, made from happy, healthy crickets grown and produced in partnership with Angka Changrit Kampuchea, our Cambodian NGO.

Changrit = cricket, and Kamleang = strong or powerful. These are the English phonetic spellings for the Khmer language words, and the meaning is immediately understood: “Nham changrit, mean kamleang!” signifies, “Eat crickets, have power!” :)

While crickets are commonly eaten in Cambodia fried and salted (and have been probably for thousands of years), Changrit Kamleang is the first cricket protein powder produced in the country.

It is made with 100% dried and milled crickets [Gryllus Bimaculatus, the best tasting crickets on the planet] from Cambodia reared specifically for human consumption on farms in the various regions surrounding Phnom Penh.  

Our crickets are fed a special diet, including fresh fruit, vegetables, rice bran, and yeast (high vitamin & protein). Feed from animals, chemicals, or antibiotics are never used.

Our farms as well as our selected partners follow the current global standards for best practices in cricket farming, and together with Angka Changrit Kampuchea our goal is to elevate the standard of production and standards of living, for all of Cambodia’s cricket farmers.

ENTOMOPHAGY 101

There is much more to come related to eating bugs!

I know, it makes me laugh too, honestly. If you’d have told me at any time in the past that one day I’d be passionately telling people to eat insects, and actually focusing my life on it, I wouldn’t have believed it either.

But the truth is I grew up on farms, growing our own food and while in Canada, hunting our own food as well. So even though I might come across as a refined city boy (ok, “refined” might be a stretch haha), deep down inside I’m kind of a country bumpkin at heart. I like the idea of sustainable self-sufficiency.

And that’s why I ended up going down the google rabbit hole in 2015 after reading the truth about insects while researching urban farming and other agricultural innovations, and got hooked on the idea that is Entomophagy.

It makes a lot of sense in terms of human nutrition, environmental sustainability, economic advancement in currently impoverished areas, etc.

Really, the only reason it seems like a “weird” idea is that westerners are conditioned through cultural biases to erroneously believe that insects are filthy and disgusting. They’re wrong. I was wrong, heck. But I changed my attitude once I learned the truth, and now I enjoy eating insects on a daily basis.

I really believe this is an industry with massive potential in so many ways, and I’m looking forward to seeing what the next few years hold.
