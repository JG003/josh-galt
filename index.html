WHO IS JOSH GALT?

I’m a former sponsored whitewater athlete and sports/lifestyle model (Nike, Adidas, Men’s Health, etc), and I’m grateful to have traveled extensively in more than 60 countries. I’ve been living as a #GlobalNomad for more than a decade now, since back in the days when staying connected while in the jungles cost a ridiculous amount of money for internet. Thankfully that’s improved!

I currently split my time between Latin America and the booming ASEAN region of Asia, with boots on the ground in as many other new locations as I can each year.

I’ve transitioned from a life of scouring the planet exploring epic whitewater rivers, to my current focus on a sector that is equally global but with exponentially more upside potential: Insects as Food, Feed, & Nutrient Upcycling.

You can follow my personal and controversial journey to optimum health and a small carbon footprint through eating a plant-based insect-rich diet, at Entovegan.com. The goal has been to create the ideal dietary framework for maximum nutrition and sustainability, by eating vegan + insects. It just may be the world’s ultimate sustainable superfood diet!

I’ve also partnered with Sibu® to launch Point68 Insect Beauty, a luxury line of skincare products combining certified organic botanical extracts with highly sustainable Soldier Grub oil, one of the world’s richest sources of the MCT lauric acid.

Previously, I spent more than a decade creating, competing in, and directing extreme outdoor events. Among the highlights: my roles as the Water Director for Primal Quest®, billed as “The World’s Most Challenging Human Endurance Competition“; as the Director of the first two Riverboarding World Championships; and as the President of the World Riverboarding Association.

After 20+ years as an athlete and pioneer in the whitewater industry, I was honored to be named as one of the “110 Outdoor Ambassadors of the Past 110 Years“ by Gear Junkie, on a list that included quite a few of my own heroes.

Check out my /now page or take a grainy scroll through some riverboarding history to learn more about me, and feel free to contact me through any social channel.

/NOW – What I’m currently working on

INSECTS – THE ULTIMATE SUSTAINABLE SUPERFOOD

We should all be eating bugs.

Technology is evolving rapidly, and despite humanity moving ever closer to the singularity, there’s another growing trend taking us back to our roots as hunters and gatherers, but with a futuristic twist: eating insects!

But wait, don’t give in to your deeply ingrained Eurocentric cultural biases and turn up your nose at insects as food just yet.

Educate yourself – they’re actually delicious, nutritious, and sustainable – and I’ll bet you’ve never had this much fun taste-testing new flavors!

Insects are also potentially the planet’s most sustainable feed source for aquaculture, poultry, and even pet food.

Take a look at the Entovegan Love #EatBugs Blog for new bug-eating inspiration you can share with your friends, buy some insects to support a good cause, and then share your culinary adventures in Entomophagy with the world!
